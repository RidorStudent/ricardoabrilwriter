Fleeting Drops by Ricardo Abril

A spontaneous project of brief texts, with no predetermined themes, started and shared from mid-pandemic year, July 2020 to October 2021. Their contents express feelings, thoughts, anecdotes, reflections, or creations of the author, current, or from some moment in his life. The author respects each and every conviction that each person holds as the driving force of their own consciousness.

Deaf, blind and dumb

Deaf to all deafness, here we go, filled with the noise of the world and indifferent to the music of life, even though the canaries and the orioles and all the known and unknown birds still sing, having survived our mad violence against everything that lives, even against ourselves...

Even though the wind continues to whistle its playful compositions... Even though the voice of our own soul persists, trying to tell us so much... Indifferent to so many voices that warn us, exhort us from so many shores...

Blind to all blindness, here we go, enraptured by the "spectacle" they put on for us every day from the outside, multicolored and fiery, but coming from the deepest darkness, indifferent not only to the subtle lights of everyday miracles but also to the most illuminated of all great miracles, undeniable: waking up alive each new day of our personal existence, as long as it lasts... Unwilling to look within and toward the essential...

Mute to all muteness, here we go...

Worldly, saturated with all worldliness, here we go, without a drop of time to soak the soul, to give it even a drink of silence, to prevent it from dehydrating or dying while alive, to allow it, from its essence, to revere and be grateful...

This is what there is, we say. And so we go, walking toward a "there" that cries, "Aye!"

Night that gives birth to dreams

At this moment, as in every moment, the dance of life is filled with multiple miracles happening simultaneously... Silence! Let nothing disturb the night: it is giving birth to dreams...

The night has just rounded the corner of yesterday and begun to walk, contemplative and smiling, along the path of today's dawn. She feels no labor pains. Birthing dreams is almost, by far, her main function, despite what others may say. And she is happy in this role, a mother so seasoned in the art of procreation and the liturgy of creation. If "The devil's knowledge is rooted in his ancient existence rather than his notorious name" imagine how much the lady night knows, ancient as she is from all antiquity...

Here and now, her labor is accompanied by rain that soaks her with its music of enamored crystals, which, in total surrender, pour themselves like a caress upon life, which seemingly sleeps.

Let them be... Let them do... Dawn will come, and many dreamers will be reaching the peak of one or several of their dreams, without imagining that the night and the rain had a hand in it. Adorable meddlers!

We are seeds

No matter how much we ignore or refuse to accept and embrace it, and no matter our notions of reality and truth, we are here because we were seeds that germinated. From the moment of conception, we began a journey of cycles that we have been fulfilling, and in our case, even as we progress from one to another and inexorably towards the final return, these cycles remain active, renew, and intersect constantly: germinate, grow, bloom, mature, return. Sometimes we stagnate, sometimes we get blocked. But life, of which we are a part, carries on with us, whether we like it or not.

As germinated seeds, active in any of the vital cycles (not chronological, but existential), even if we ignore or evade it, our vocation is to grow and bloom in the concert of life, multiplying the material and spiritual gifts that keep us standing and that renew, each new day, our possibility to continue learning, sharing, and growing.

The dynamics of knowing how to give and how to receive from others set the tone and height of our intimate evolution.

Life my great love

As I once said, and as I strive to continue practicing, life is for me a great love. And within that love, intimately coexist multiple and also great loves; for the rain, for example. Also for the wind, the fire, the earth, humanity, the cosmos, the small yet magnificent daily miracles that life constantly grants us, many of which we do not even perceive, but they are there, blossoming tirelessly.

Our sensitivity falters. Life does not falter.

A Veils and Sleepless Nights

There are many recurring sleepless nights, where the soul searches for itself in despair. Such sleeplessness persists, especially in these times filled with dark events, so much bloodshed, and such vile fire, destroying all optimism and nearly all hope. The night remains, if not for everyone, at least for many, an impeccable and relentless mirror of our deepest and most authentic existential anxieties.

It is inevitable, here and now, to ruminate on impotence, fear, and pessimism about what the world is experiencing. And perhaps, at least for some, to also ruminate on shame, for the share of responsibility that falls upon us and which the mirror of events rubs into our hearts. Before our own conscience, we are never immune, even though we practice such diverse forms of evasion, conformity, and complicity…
