Septentenas by Ricardo Abril

Acceptance of brevity as a literary and philosophical value, to capture the attention of a world rushing from one place to another. Capsules of 70 words, invariably challenging the reader's attention, perception, comprehension, self-reflection, and critique.

Because feeling and thinking will always be worthwhile, these 70-word texts offer reflections intended to mobilize sensitivity, a readiness for contemplation, and a healthy critical sense in relation to individual and social human events and activities.

Septentena #6

We are ALMOST NOTHING in front of EVERYTHING, yet we find it easy to believe we are the best of everything. We know almost nothing but think we know it all. We are insensitive and indifferent to the gravest human problems but boast about “feeling.” We hardly know ourselves but think we know others well, judging everyone here and there. Such arrogance!

Septentena #10

Yes. The tongue can caress or kill. It is versatile and very ductile to human psyche and spirituality. When speaking, what we carry inside comes out, the good and the bad. More of one or the other, depending on our choice. Especially because we don't think much about what we pronounce and are very unguarded about silencing our impulses when they want to become -- and become -- treacherous daggers...

Septentena #13

We have art to avoid dying, in life, from the many vile ways the modern world uses to annihilate us... To reciprocate the infinite essential love of life... To say the unsaid, show the unseen, imagine and share the unimagined. To create whatever is possible to create in this never-ending search for meaning until the curtain falls. For everyone, always.

Septentena #15

In this reality of our country, so full of grim events, so much spilled blood, so much vile fire destroying all optimism and hope, it's inevitable to ruminate impotence, fear, pessimism. And perhaps, for some, also ruminate shame for the share of responsibility that belongs to us and that the mirror of events rubs in our hearts and our indifference. And who doesn't feel the sting?

Septentena #19

Thinking and feeling about that culturally valued cliché of “risking one's life” for something, whatever it is, I felt that those of us privileged to live beyond sixty, now definitely “turning the corner,” find ourselves surprised “risking our death,” meaning, aware of the irremediable closeness, sooner rather than later, we are betting on “living well to die well.” Each in their own way. I believe.

Septentena #20

To SEE in a drop of dew trembling on the edge of a grateful leaf, one of the many strokes of perfection that nature constantly donates to our souls to be enjoyed reverently. To SEE in the face of a hungry, abandoned child, with a gaze lost in dark nothingness, all the vile indifference we have reached in this uncivilized and ill-conceived "civilization."

Septentena #24

To believe, from any corner of faith, that we are the authentic children of God and others perhaps birthed by the Devil... To judge, from a self-conceded supreme moral authority, the acts, feelings, and stories of others... To look away when the tragedy of others, near and far, challenges us... To demand and demand, almost never to yield or concede... To always complain, for yes and no... Almost never to be grateful... How many faces does our ridiculous arrogance have?

Septentena #25

Love me, I am life, singing still in your present. Love me, I am death, your permanent companion. Love me, I am time, for your good or bad fortune. Love me, I am love, the one that always makes you strong. Love me, I am oblivion, which makes evil absent. Love me, I am silence, music in your soul, peace in your mind. Love me, I am your story, recognize me, be brave. Bet on me, I will always be your opportunity.

Septentena #27

Sometimes a lot of time is little. Sometimes eternity fits in an instant. In matters of love, little doesn't fit. We say a lot, practice little. We judge a lot, understand little. We babble a lot, listen little or nothing. We ask a lot, give little or nothing... We cover a lot, squeeze little... We complain a lot, act very little... Sometimes the little serves us a lot, sometimes the much drives us crazy... We are a lot. We are little.

Septentena #29

Fragile, yes, but incorruptible. It has been, by far, the greatest victim of all kinds of attacks, millions of them perpetrated, cold-bloodedly and consciously, in the very souls, even of the most optimistic, by force of seemingly irredeemable realities. It has died so many times, in me, in you, in almost everyone! But it survives and insists on summoning us, knowing that we are nothing without it. Authentic hope requires action, incorruptible.

Septentena #30

Only some children are cared for, with the soul invested. Why is it not so for all the children of the world? We evade a lot on this matter, but here our political conscience plays immensely. And our subtle violences against them, putting barriers to the full expression of their being. And if we are like this with our own, how much apathy and indifference do we exercise towards the tragedy of all the others?

Septentena #31

Each soul knows how much it hurts inside, irremediably, with the passage of time, to regret having regretted before doing something that represented vital flight and transcendence, but we declined, cowed by perverse fears installed from outside or, worse, self-inflicted. Although sometimes open doors are scarce, life always gives us windows to look beyond and fly. But it demands belief in our wings. And the will to act.

Septentena #38

Dreaming costs nothing for those who, the majority, see it as mere illusion. But there is a dreaming that connects many human beings, aware of the extinction threats generated by extreme greed and general, also extreme, political insensitivity. It costs a lot because indifference and apathy are growing. While wealth grows for a few, total misery looms over all the rest of us.

Septentena #39

I am not the only one who has felt it nor the only one who has said it. We are made of light and shadow. Sometimes we float in high dimensions, impregnated with cosmic euphoria, almost angels, almost gods. Suddenly, sometimes, a sudden vertigo leads us to the deepest shadow. And from there we emerge, with new lessons and new light, grown, almost angels, almost gods. The shadow lurks. The light triumphs...

Septentena #43

Conceived, in most cases, still today, unwanted, we come into the world, an undesirable one that clumsy humanity has previously built. A bitter reality, against which rebelling is a tormenting, tormented, and almost useless path. Only almost. Because many, despite everything, keep the breath of necessary rebellion and indispensable action for dignity. We curse its planting. We enjoy, indignantly, its invaluable harvest for all.

Septentena #47

Silence does not always grant. At the bottom of certain silences, there can be disdain, neglect, insensitivity. Sometimes one stays silent to do the opposite of granting: to strip value from what is heard, seen, or read; there silence is a short-bladed weapon. That wounds and bleeds, sheltered in its own shadow. Perhaps I am a bird shooting at the shotgun of indifference. Am I not?

Septentena #48

Sometimes words become slippery… they do not lend themselves to letting us say, they find no accommodation, they come and go before you can grasp and use them. They dance, mysterious, in those chatty silences of your sleeplessness, but they do not grant you a dance… they get stuck in your throat when an emotion urges them; they behave "elusive"… they have fun playing "stay at the tip of the tongue." Yes, words have their own story!

Septentena #49

Yes, there are evils that last a hundred years, and many more in some cases. And yes, there are peoples who endure them. Who tolerate them as if nothing, as if they had a lifelong visa in the collective conscience. And rivers of blood and human misery flow. Peoples where executioners enjoy consent and public prestige. Where any hope of historical justice is suspicious, discredited, even if legitimate. “By their brutes, you shall know them”…
