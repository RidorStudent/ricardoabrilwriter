    Fraternal Invitation

    In these virtual channels, windows that are now used for almost everything, I will share with you, fellow travelers of the same universe in which I was born and live, some sparks of my consciousness. With human fraternity and hope… and with all due respect.

    I reaffirm that I consider myself solely as a craftsman and sower, in my own way. I hope that in your wandering souls, some of these sparks/seeds will find fertile ground, germinate, and bloom… We know we are connected by many threads of different kinds. Beyond all that allows us to create the illusion of distance, we share the same heartbeats, driven by similar passions, touched by the same mysteries, and awed by the same miracles that life endlessly repeats. No matter how dormant we may sometimes feel, even for long periods, we can always awaken anew and drink from its nourishing source—finding reasons and motivations to uplift our spirits and continue our search. To keep discovering…

    Ricardo Abril's Biography

    Having worked since the age of 7, I set out to learn from the world, from people, and from various engaging trades, experiencing the different flavors and colors of existence. I live enamored with the wind and dreams and the constant awakening and blooming of life with each new dawn.

    Fortunately, I never lost my childhood curiosity and wonder, my love for exploring everything, asking questions, conversing, and sharing simply for the joyous pleasure it brings. When I began teaching, I realized that the only way to truly connect with children was to rediscover my own inner child. This led me on an intimate exploration where most of my children's poems blossomed. The sensitivity of the children inspired me to continue down this path.

    I love poetry. Most of the children's poems I have written were discovered between 1975 and 1984, some nearly 50 years ago. They were born and have grown and traveled many paths to pay humble tribute to the magnificent miracle of life and the immense gift and responsibility of being human, of sharing with all of you and with all those who came before, those who are here now, and those who will come after, the privilege of feeling and dreaming. And of being able to express it.

    The other texts correspond to different moments and transits of my personal history and aim to mobilize sensitivities and awareness on themes and issues that the vertiginous urgency of contemporary life leaves aside in function of the haste and efficiency imposed by the model of society we are sharing.

    In the mission that I am about to share, everything I have tried to write and share is framed.

    About the Website

    Mission: To be a mirror window that offers paths of observation, contemplation, and reflection inward into consciousness and outward in the coherence of attitudes and actions.

    Vision: To sustain the vigor and warmth of human engagement through respectful questioning and provocation over time.

    Children's poetry

    Simple texts, rich in poetic language, that have wandered for several years through classrooms, homes, family events, social, cultural, and community gatherings, and that aim to reach more hearts that love art and life. They recreate everyday themes and situations in a combined manner with proposals from fantasy and with deep respect for children's sensitivity, being provocative, recitable, and representable. They vindicate the flight of fantasy, sensitivity, and creativity of the child's soul and all sensitive hearts.

    Fleeting drops

    A spontaneous project of brief texts, with no predetermined themes, started and shared from mid-pandemic year, July 2020 to October 2021. Their contents express feelings, thoughts, anecdotes, reflections, or creations of the author, current, or from some moment in his life. The author respects each and every conviction that each person holds as the driving force of their own consciousness.

    Septentenas

    Because the author is now approaching 70 years old and because feeling-thinking is always worthwhile, reflections are proposed in 70-word texts called Septentenas. Their intention is to mobilize sensitivity, the disposition for contemplation, and a healthy critical sense in relation to individual and social human events and activities. This project started in November 2021 and is still in production and dissemination.
